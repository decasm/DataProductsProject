Fuel Cost Comparison App

Pitch

Devon Smith

Basic Idea

  1. Miles per gallon (mpg) is a poor choice for comparing cars
  2. Gallons per mile (gpm) or Cost per mile (cpm) are better
  3. GPM Sheds Light On Why Hybrid Cars Are Tough To Justify On Fuel Savings Alone
  4. Miles-Per-Gallon Is Just Stupid. No, Really, It Is.

Plots

The relationship is not linear. As MPG increase to 20, 30, 40, etc, the amount of fuel used (GPM) doesn't change nearly as much.

And Cost per mile is better

While Gallons per mile is a good, actually showing people what it costs them is even better. Allowing people to use their local fuel prices to figure out what they have to pay allows them to make better choices. And also allows different fuels to be accounted for - regular, plus, premium grades, and diesel.

My Solution

A simple app that does some simple math that gives better information for better decision making.

Cost Per Mile Calculator

Future iterations could include geolocating the user and auto-filling in the average fuel cost in the area.
